My studies

As a cultural anthropologist/ethnologist I am curious about: peoples life and stories; what they do and why; how they perceive them and the world around them; and how they give them all meaning. I talk to people and visit/travel places to gain an understanding of peoples life, culture and society. For this purpose, I have several tools, theoretical knowledge and practical experiences in fieldwork. And at the end, I want to tell and present these stories about peoples life, culture and society.

City_Nature on the Mur


Rebuilding processes of urban riverscapes and natural landscapes in Graz in history and present

In 2020, the riverscape in the city of Graz is being reconstructed into local recreation areas. Together with this reconstruction of the riverscape an urban development process is happening in Graz, which also can be observed in other cities. What are the reasons for this trend in the urban development of reconstructing urban riverscapes, and building local recreation areas along rivers? The initial hypothesis for answering this question is the conception of the so-called River History, whereby the reasons go along with processes in the history of the urban industrial society, which had transformed natural rivers into Industrialized Rivers. Due to processes in the history of industrialization and urbanization, nature has been primarily considered as a utilizable resource for industry, and has been externalized to a place outside the city.
Today cities are increasingly not building anymore against nature, but with nature. I will show the contemporary process of urban development with nature by using the example of the new urban riverscape in Graz. At the river Mur in Graz in 2020, there is an urban development against and with nature taking place at the same time. This process can be seen as the construction of a ‚second‘ nature (Lefebvre) where nature is being integrated into the city as a design element of the urban space. Therefore the ‚second‘ nature in the city is both: a material nature, which plays an important role for the life in the city, but also a cultural artefact, which evokes nature.

Floating the river Sava


Exploring the river Sava in Croatia by a-self made float

The river Sava connects on her 990 kilometres four countries, three capital cities and about 8 million people. Geographically, the River marks the beginning of the Balkans. But for the people along the Sava, the river is the heart of the Balkans and a unique natural environment. So we – four discoverers – hit the water route to discover this ‚european Amazonas‘.
We travelled with a self-made float made of wood and plastic barrels. In the end of the summer period, the water level of the Sava is low, and the river is navigable by a float. Aside from periods of low water, the Sava overflows the region, called Posavina, and transforms into a torrential river. For this reason, the people have learned not to underestimate the river, but to appreciate Nature. During our two-weeks trip, we also learned to appreciate the Sava. Her leisureliness, loneliness and peace stimulate us to reflect and to dream. For sure this was not the last adventure for the float called S.M.S. Sunflower and her crew, and so this first 100 kilometres, from Zagreb to Blinjski Kut, were just the beginning, not the end.

Reconstructing Augarten


Insights behind the fences

The Augarten park is a popular place for the people in Graz, especially for the residents of the neighbourhoods of Jakomini and Gries. The park is located in the middle of the city. Next to the park, the river Mur is floating through the city, which divides Graz economically, socially and historically into two different parts. When visiting the Augarten in February 2019, the park is changing: At the north end of the park fences were raised, and there can be heard noises from construction works and motor saws. Because today is the day when parts of the Augarten were transformed and rebuild into a new recreation area. A new river bay should upgrade the park and increase its popularity with a new riverside and new benches and new possibilities for consumption.
But first, the trees along the river have to be removed. It is noisy work because motor saws and power shovels are cutting and removing the trees. The falling trees in Graz became a symbol of resentment. Because two years ago the construction works for the new, controversial, power plant in the river Mur started. And for this new power plant, it was necessary to cut the trees and to remove the nature in the city, to be able to rebuild a new nature.

Abandoned Places


Another world is waiting just behind the door.

"First of all, there is this thrill: you are getting closer to the building, you are nervous, and you have no idea if there will be a security guy or not. You are looking for an entrance, you climb the wall or get under the fence. And when you find it, you pass a threshold and get from one world to another. It is a thrilling feeling because you know that this one building felt out from time, and has some stories to tell about the past, the present and the future. The past is the history of the building; the present you can see in the murals, the items and legacy from other visitors; and in soon in the future, this places will be removed. The city is changing all the time, and so these places are also changing and will be vanished soon."

'Afghani Routes': On the Way in Iran


Diary notes from a journey in Iran.

"For the first time during this journey, I am doing a walk alone. It is a strange feeling to walk the streets without any company. The sun is shining, the weather is warm and I am realizing that the people in the neighbourhood are looking at me with an interesting view – do I really look so strange to them? Some of them are turning around for me, some are staring at me and my way of walking. These people, who are looking at me, are men. The women, mostly covered in black cloaks, are walking faster than the men do, and are just looking at me for a moment – or they even do not look at me but covering their face. I can see this special gaze quite often here in Iran, and I am wondering if they are starring because I look so strange, or if the men always stare like this at other people. It is a lazy gaze: Men are sitting in front of the shop, or are queuing in front of the bakery, waiting long but talking hardly. They are playing with their prayers chains and are looking up from time to time."

One Night on Train


Atmospheres between sunset and sunrise.

A night train is more than just a place for transit, the train does not only transport people from one place to another. A night train also enables us to experience the time between sunset and sunrise in a special way: On night trains we are travelling together with unknown people, we can unroll and share our thoughts, and we can feel the rumble of the wheels intensely. The night on a train is a vanishing place where we can meet random people, and share stories and time together. Or we can just listen to the sound of driving wheels and fall into sleep until we wake up in the morning in a different city or different country.

The football stadium


Built and experienced space aside from the 90 minutes

Our experience of football stadiums mostly occurs within the 90 minutes of a football match. We experience the football stadium with two teams on the field and an audience at the terraces. But what kind of atmospheres we can find, when the lights are off, the songs from the terraces trail away and the last person on the field is the green-keeper? Stadiums have their own architecture, and they are embedded in special contexts within the city and the district they are built. The main function of the stadium is the football match, but they also have a life beside that.

Berlin: Cuvrystraße


The Cuvry wasteland. An informal settlement as a form of resistance.

Since the fall of the Berlin wall, the city has changed, and especially the district Kreuzberg. Not all the people in Kreuzberg agree to these changes. Let's have a walk: We are stepping out from the subway station Schlesisches Tor. People here in the streets are talking in different languages. We are heading on the Schlesische Straße, we are passing pubs, clubs and restaraunts. We see two about 15 meters large murals on the walls. Attracted from these murals, we are entering a wasteland area, which is enclosed by a fence and bushes. When stepping in, we are escaping from the noisy Schlesische Straße and we find ourselves in another world: at the Cuvry wasteland. This area is a living space, where people have built an informal village in the middle of the city. But also a place of resistance, fighting against gentrification in Kreuzberg.

Trans-Siberian Railway


A journey by train, far away from home.

I am standing on the train platform at Kilometer number 5.290. In Russia, the length specification along the tracks represents the distance from this specific place to the capital Moscow. I am waiting here for my train, which takes me back vom Lake Baikal to the city of Irkutsk. Before, I walked with Brian, the vagabond from Wyoming, for two hours through the forest, and for one more hour along the tracks, until we reach this platform here. We meet each other in Angasolka, a small village with about 30 inhabitants, and where once a week a small train brings commodities for daily life. Our train is going to come in half an hour. But we can hear already another train coming through the forest. Brian jumps on the tracks, puts a Rubel coin on it, and jumps back to the platform. It is a freight train, which drives by with noise. It is a huge freight train, as I saw them many times here in Sibiria in the last three weeks. As the train passed by and disappears in the forest again, Brian jumps again on the tracks and picks up his coin. The coin became very thin.
