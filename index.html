Studies

Cultural Anthropology

Cultural Anthropology/Ethnology observes the world around us. As ethnologist we try to understand how the world is composite, how people are living, what they are doing, how the they are looking at the world and how they are giving her a meaning.
The ethnologist travels into the world to understand the ‚other‘, but mostly he comes back with new knowledge about himself and his own ‚culture‘. This is why the Cultural Anthropology is a self-reflexive science: She is looking at the world and reality as created by people, and she realizes the people als involved with the world and the reality. This creating is the main interest of the Cultural Anthropology: culture. For this kind of approach the ethnologist has several tools: to look with the own eyes on a research field and to talk with the people; to realize and reflect the own position as an researcher inside the field; to so the field, the people and their actions divided into small sections, but also to see it in a context of a big picture; to interpret the seen/unseen and to put it in a context of theory, history, culture and society.
The ethnologist wants to tell stories about the people, the field and the world, and comes back from the fieldwork with a message. With every single fieldwork-experience the ethnologist gains new perceptions about the world and about himself. This new perceptions are what we want to experience, to interpret and to acquire, for the reason to ask ourself some questions. Because the questions are essential for how we look at the world. Those new questions arise if we deal with different and surprising people and outlooks, and hence if we escape from our own little world filled with things we already know and we are expecting. At the same time we learn: Trueness is not an idea about the world, but rather what keeps us in touch with the world. Therefore the ethnologist realizes that not he is the one who disassembles a trueness, but the trueness disassembles him.

Digital Humanities

Digital Humanities are the extension of the principles of the Humanities and Cultural Anthropology by digital methods and inside a digital world. On the one hand there is a focus on new possibiliets and tools. A student of Digital Humanities immerges into the field of computer science, programming, information and data visualisation, web development and so on. Thereby he is able to look at, to analyse, to interprete and to present cultural data in a new and other way. That leads to new methodoligal and theoretic approachs, which extend the scientific profil of the Humanities.
On the other hand the ‚digital cultural anthropologist‘ focus on further reaserch fields and questions, respectively he is able to see the interlacing between online/offline and digital/analog. The interests of the digital ethnologist are not only people and cultures in a digital field (‚Digital Anthropology‘ & ‚Virtual ethnography‘) but rather he is acting too inside a digital field and with digital tools. He has knowledge about this tools, he knows the field which he is working in and for this reason the can develop the field for his own requirements. The occupation with bits and bytes, programming languages and computer science especially increase his own thinking in an networked, structured and analytic way as well as his skills in troubleshooting.
The main advantages of the 'digital cultural anthropologist' are the combination of principles, skills and methods from the Humanities, as well as his own acting, working and get-along inside the field, which is affected through digitalization and innovative methods/tools. Although computer science, programming and communication technology are not obvious the main interests of an ethnologist, he can exactly there use his strengths: flexibility, willingness for advancement, informations kills, creativity, patience and visions.

Abandoned Places


Abandoned Places: The another world is waiting just behind the door.
"First of all there is this thrill: you are getting closer to the building, you are nervous and you have no idea if there will be a security guy. You are looking for a entrance, you climb the wall or get under the fence. Suddenly you calm down – within one jump you get from one world to another. It is a nice feeling. [...] And you know that this special building felt out from time and has some storys to tell. And you know that this are the places, where you can find the past, the present and the future. The past ist the histroy of the building; the present you can find in the murals, the items and legacy from other visitors and the other visitors you can meet there; future, because you know that this places will be removed soon. The citiy is changing all the time, and so this places will be closed, you wont be able to enter anymore, their will be destructed or are loosing the special thrill."

'Afghani Routes': On the Way in Iran


Diary notes about a travel with A. in Iran.
For the first time in this journey I am doing a walk for myself. It is a strange feeling to walk the streets without any company. The sun is shining, the weather is warm and I am realizing that the people in the neighbourhood are looking at me with an interested but strange view – do I really look so strange for them? Some of them are turning around for me, some are staring at me and my way of walking. This people, who are looking at me, are men. The women, mostly covered in black cloaks, are walking faster then the men do and are just looking at me for a moment – or they even dont look at me but are covering the face. I can see this look quite often here in Iran and I am wondering, if they are starring because I look so strange or if the men always stare like this at other people. It is a lazy look. Men are sitting in front of the shop, or are queing in front of the bakery. They are playing with their prayers chains and are looking left and right from time to time.

One Night on Train


Notice and talks between sunset and sunrise.
A nighttrain is more than just a night-time-place for transit and movement. A train does not only transport people from one place to another, but the night train also enables us to experience the period between sunset and sunrise in a different way. On night trains we are travelling together with people. We can listen their storys and we can tell them our stories, we can unroll and share our thoughs and we can feel the rumble of the weels. The night on train is the period and the place in which we can meet other people and share stories. Or we can just listen to the sound of driving wheels and fall into sleep, until we wake up at morning in an different city or different country.

Berlin: Cuvrystraße


The Cuvry waste land. An informal settlment as a form of resistance.

Since the fall of the Berlin wall things have changed in Berlin and the district Kreuzberg – and not all people are happy with this changes. We are stepping out form the station Schlesisches Tor, above our heads the train is passing while people are talking in different languages. We are heading on the Schlesische Straße, we are passing pubs, clubs and resteraunts. Above the club Lido a mural is letting know the people, what Kreuzberg thinks about the so called gentrification and the new guests. On the other street side there are two 15 meters large murals. Attracted from the murals we keep on walking. A few steps further we are escaping from the noisy Schlesische Straße and we find ourselvs in another world: at the Cuvry waste land. The chaos, the garbage and the inhabitans there also want to tell the world the same message, as the three words from the mural wants to tell.

ĆevapĆiĆi


Post-migrant practieses of positioning on the basis of Ćevapčići. An contrapuntual, and thereby an unexpected view at the phenomena of the ‚Yugo-Fešta‘.
With an understanding for so called ‚post-migrant theories‘ – and the process of delocalization, new-localization, ambiguity and liminal biographies, the in-between – we can look more positiv at these produced equivocality and alienation and we can see it as an resource for how we look at certain phenomenas. This approach can realize the equivocality, the hybrid-identities and the break with an binary logic of identity-construction as an opportunity for looking at phenomenas with a other perspective as they appear to us. By asking different questions we get a different view. Especially we can escape from expectations and confirmation of expectations, because we can see, what nobody is expecting. Quite often we meet this expectations and conformations, where we rise questions about the the native and the strange.
